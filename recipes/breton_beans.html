Breton beans

Description

Bean a la bretonne is a filling dish of white beans stewed in an aromatic tomato sauce with bacon and sausage. It is a traditional dish of Polish cuisine, ideal for colder days.

Ingredients

  • 400 g dry beans or 2 cans of beans
  • 250 g bacon (steamed or smoked)
  • 250 g sausage
  • 1 large onion
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • 1 can sliced tomatoes + 2 tbsp tomato paste*
  • 3 allspices
  • bay leaf
  • 2 flat tbsp dried marjoram

Steps

  1. Put the dry beans in a pot, pour cold water (about 10 cm above the level of the beans) and set aside to soak overnight.

  2. The next day, drain, pour in 1 liter of fresh water, add 1 teaspoon of salt, allspice and bay leaf and cook covered for about 1 and 1/2 hours until tender.

  3. Cut the skin off the bacon, dice the rest. Put it in a frying pan and, stirring every now and then, fry it thoroughly, transfer it to the pot with beans (or to the canned beans boiled in the pot with the marinade).

  4. Thoroughly fry the diced sausage on the rendered fat (peel it beforehand if necessary), add the diced onion and fry for about 5 minutes more. At the end, add grated garlic.

  5. Transfer to the pot with the beans and cook for about 5 minutes on higher heat without a lid.

  6. Add sliced canned tomatoes and tomato paste. Season with marjoram, salt and freshly ground pepper. Cook for about 15 minutes, enjoy!
